    Projet final

    Game principle


    Managing "Chez Tonton", a mexican food truck

    3 different dishes :
    Tacos, Burritos, Nachos

    3 differents types of customers, each one orders a different dish :
    The Elf orders Nachos, the Skeleton orders a Taco, the Goblin orders a Burrito

    The customers appear in waves on a different location, among three possible. They wait a while to be served. If they are not served until their waiting time is over, they leave without food.
    Each location can be occupied by a customer or not for each wave of clients.


    Each dish has a different preparation time, so it is not always possible to prepare them all before the clients leave.

    The burning fire on the frying pan indicates that Tonton is preparing the dish for the client marked by the green check icon.


    Every time a dish is sold to a customer, we earn money. The price is specific to each dish, and can vary according to the disposal of stocks (which is represented by the clock).




    The time is shown by the green crab on bottom left (with the clock).



    Training

    Q-learning :

    Matrix Q(s,a) size : 192(states)*6(actions)

    Three different versions of the game

  • Version n°1:
    Only one dish is served for each wave of clients, does not depend on the preparation time, no price evolution
    What the IA has to learn :
    price of a taco > price of a burrito > price of Nachos, for each wave.

  • Version n°2:
    Multiple dishes can be served during each wave of clients. The prices are fixed during the whole day. What the IA has to learn :
    price of 3 Nachos > 1 Tacos = 1 Nachos + 1 Burritos > 2 Nachos > 1 Burritos > 1 Nachos

  • Version n°3:
    Multiple dishes can be served during each wave of clients. The prices change depending on the time of the day.
    What the IA has to learn :
    see the table above for the order of profits + "save" some dishes in order to serve them later
